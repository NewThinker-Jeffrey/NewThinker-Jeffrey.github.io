A brief introduction to algebra and derivation

Algebra (over a field/ring)

algebra over a field
An algebra over a field KKK (often simply called an algebra) is a vector space AAA equipped with a bilinear product "⋅\cdot⋅", i.e. for w,x,y,z∈Aw,x,y,z\in Aw,x,y,z∈A, α,β∈K\alpha,\beta\in Kα,β∈K, we have

  • (w+x)⋅(y+z)=w⋅y+w⋅z+x⋅y+x⋅z(w+x)\cdot (y+z)=w\cdot y+w\cdot z + x\cdot y + x\cdot z(w+x)⋅(y+z)=w⋅y+w⋅z+x⋅y+x⋅z
  • (αx)⋅(βy)=αβ(x⋅y)(\alpha x)\cdot (\beta y)=\alpha\beta (x\cdot y)(αx)⋅(βy)=αβ(x⋅y)

We might omit the symbol "⋅\cdot⋅" if the context is clear.

generalization: algebra over a ring
Replacing the field KKK of scalars by a commutative ring RRR leads to the more general notion of an algebra over a ring. Note now the algebra itself is assumed to be an RRR-module (instead of a vector space over KKK).

Associative algebra

An algebra is an associative algebra if in addition:

  • (x⋅y)⋅z=x⋅(y⋅z)(x\cdot y)\cdot z=x\cdot (y\cdot z)(x⋅y)⋅z=x⋅(y⋅z)

Examples:

  • The tensor algebra
  • The exterior algebra

A commutative algebra is an associative algebra that has a commutative multiplication, or, equivalently, an associative algebra that is also a commutative ring.

Graded algebra

Graded ring

A graded ring RRR is a ring such that the underlying additive group is a direct sum of abelian groups {Ri}i∈I{\displaystyle \{R_{i}\}_{i\in I}}{Ri​}i∈I​ such that RiRj⊆Ri+j{\displaystyle R_{i}R_{j}\subseteq R_{i+j}}Ri​Rj​⊆Ri+j​.
The index set III is usually the set of nonnegative integers or the set of integers, but can be any monoid (幺半群).

Graded algebra
An algebra AAA over a ring RRR is a graded algebra if it is graded as a ring.
In the usual case where the ring RRR is not graded (in particular if RRR is a field), it is given the trivial grading (every element of RRR is of degree 0). Thus, R⊆A0{\displaystyle R\subseteq A_{0}}R⊆A0​ and the graded pieces Ai{\displaystyle A_{i}}Ai​ are R-modules.

In the case where the ring RRR is also a graded ring, then one requires that

RiAj⊆Ai+j{\displaystyle R_{i}A_{j}\subseteq A_{i+j}}Ri​Aj​⊆Ai+j​
In other words, we require AAA to be a graded left module over RRR.

Graded commutative algebra

graded commutative ring
a graded-commutative ring (also called a skew-commutative ring) is a graded ring that is commutative in the graded sense; that is, homogeneous elements x, y satisfy
xy=(−1)∣x∣∣y∣yx{\displaystyle xy=(-1)^{|x||y|}yx}xy=(−1)∣x∣∣y∣yx,
where ∣x ∣|x |∣x ∣ and ∣y ∣|y |∣y ∣ denote the degrees of xxx and yyy.

A commutative (non-graded) ring, with trivial grading, is a basic example.
An exterior algebra is an example of a graded-commutative ring that is not commutative in the non-graded sense. 外代数不是commutative的，即一般 η∧ψ≠ψ∧η\eta\wedge \psi\ne \psi\wedge\etaη∧ψ=ψ∧η；但作为一个分级代数，它是graded-commutative的。

Commutator

The commutator gives an indication of the extent to which a certain binary operation fails to be commutative.
There are different definitions used in group theory and ring theory.

Commutator for a groups
The commutator of two elements, ggg and hhh, of a group GGG, is the element
[g,h]=g−1h−1gh[g, h] = g^{−1}h^{−1}gh[g,h]=g−1h−1gh.
But many group theorists might also define the commutator as
[g,h]=ghg−1h−1[g, h] = ghg^{−1}h^{−1}[g,h]=ghg−1h−1

However, both of the two definition can give an indication of the extent to which the group operation fails to be commutative.

Commutator for rings or associative algebras
The commutator of two elements aaa and bbb of a ring RRR (including any associative algebra) is defined by
[a,b]=ab−ba.{\displaystyle [a,b]=ab-ba.}[a,b]=ab−ba.

Commutator for graded rings and algebras
When dealing with graded algebras, the commutator is usually replaced by the graded commutator, defined in homogeneous components as

Derivation on an algebra

derivation: linear + Leibniz's law

Given an algebra AAA over a ring or a field KKK, a derivation is a K-linear map D:A→AD : A \rightarrow AD:A→A that satisfies Leibniz's law:

  • Leibniz's law: D(ab)=aD(b)+D(a)b.{\displaystyle D(ab)=aD(b)+D(a)b.}D(ab)=aD(b)+D(a)b.

如果把AAA看做流形上ppp点处的光滑函数芽组成的向量空间Cp∞(M)C_p^\infty(M)Cp∞​(M)（两个函数芽之间的加法和乘法定义为在它们定义域交集内的逐点相加和相乘），那Cp∞(M)C_p^\infty(M)Cp∞​(M)上的derivation就是一个切向量。这正是切向量的导数定义。
如果把AAA看做流形上的光滑函数层C∞(M)C^\infty(M)C∞(M)（两个函数之间的加法和乘法定义为在它们定义域交集内的逐点相加和相乘），那C∞(M)C^\infty(M)C∞(M)上的derivation就是一个切向量场。每个光滑切向量场都是一个derivation，这是容易看到的；但"每个derivation都对应一个光滑切向量场"就不是那么显然了，这需要些证明。

Vector valued differential forms

E-valued differential forms

Let M be a smooth manifold and E → M be a smooth vector bundle over M. We denote the space of smooth sections of a bundle E by Γ(E).
An E-valued differential form of degree ppp is a smooth section of the tensor product bundle of E with Λp(T ∗M), the p-th exterior power of the cotangent bundle of M. The space of such forms is denoted by

Ωp(M,E)=Γ(E⊗ΛpT∗M).{\displaystyle \Omega ^{p}(M,E)=\Gamma (E\otimes \Lambda ^{p}T^{*}M).}Ωp(M,E)=Γ(E⊗ΛpT∗M).
Because Γ is a strong monoidal functor, this can also be interpreted as
Γ(E⊗ΛpT∗M)=Γ(E)⊗Ω0(M)Γ(ΛpT∗M)=Γ(E)⊗Ω0(M)Ωp(M),{\displaystyle \Gamma (E\otimes \Lambda ^{p}T^{*}M)=\Gamma (E)\otimes _{\Omega ^{0}(M)}\Gamma (\Lambda ^{p}T^{*}M)=\Gamma (E)\otimes _{\Omega ^{0}(M)}\Omega ^{p}(M),}Γ(E⊗ΛpT∗M)=Γ(E)⊗Ω0(M)​Γ(ΛpT∗M)=Γ(E)⊗Ω0(M)​Ωp(M),
where the latter two tensor products are the tensor product of modules over the ring Ω0(M) of smooth R-valued functions on M (see the seventh example here).
Equivalently, an E-valued differential form can be defined as a bundle morphism
TM⊗⋯⊗TM→E{\displaystyle TM\otimes \cdots \otimes TM\to E}TM⊗⋯⊗TM→E
which is totally skew-symmetric.

E-valued 0-form

By convention, an E-valued 0-form is just a section of the bundle E. That is,
Ω0(M,E)=Γ(E). {\displaystyle \Omega ^{0}(M,E)=\Gamma (E).\,}Ω0(M,E)=Γ(E).

V-valued differential forms

Let V be a fixed vector space. A V-valued differential form of degree p is a differential form of degree p with values in the trivial bundle M × V.
The space of such forms is denoted Ωp(M, V).

Ordinary differential forms can be viewed as R\mathbb{R}R-valued differential forms

When V = R one recovers the definition of an ordinary differential form. If V is finite-dimensional, then one can show that the natural homomorphism
Ωp(M)⊗RV→Ωp(M,V),{\displaystyle \Omega ^{p}(M)\otimes _{\mathbb {R} }V\to \Omega ^{p}(M,V),}Ωp(M)⊗R​V→Ωp(M,V),
where the first tensor product is of vector spaces over R, is an isomorphism.

Exterior derivetive for vector valued differential forms

V-valued differential forms naturally inherit the exterior derivetive. (choose a base, and get the ordinary exterior derivetive of the commponents) .
But general E-valued differential forms not, which need extra structure (a connection).
